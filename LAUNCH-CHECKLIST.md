# Launching the Brand — Everything You Actually Need

A founder's checklist for a single-estate Croatian plum brandy (šljivovica), aimed at a
premium / export-capable launch. Croatia = EU, so you're inside the EU single market.

> ⚠️ Not legal advice. Alcohol is one of the most regulated products there is. Confirm every
> licensing/tax point with a Croatian accountant + the customs office (Carinska uprava) before
> producing for sale.

---

## 1. The thing in the bottle (production)

- [ ] **Decide: distill yourself vs. contract-distill.** Easiest start: have a licensed Lika
      distillery distill *your* estate plums to *your* spec, then you bottle/brand. Lets you sell
      legally fast without building a distillery.
- [ ] Confirm the **orchard yield** and plum variety (Bistrica/Požegača is the classic šljivovica plum).
- [ ] Lock the **production spec**: ferment time, single vs. double distillation (*prepečenica*),
      oak aging (Slavonian oak), target ABV per SKU.
- [ ] **Batch + bottling records** — premium buyers and regulators both want traceability.

## 2. Legal & tax (Croatia / EU)

- [ ] Register a **company** (d.o.o. or obrt) — talk to a Croatian accountant about which.
- [ ] **Excise / trošarina** registration with Carinska uprava (alcohol is excise goods).
- [ ] **Excise warehouse / authorised warehousekeeper** status if you store/move duty-suspended stock.
- [ ] **Food-business registration** (alcohol = food product) + HACCP for bottling.
- [ ] **Excise stamps / banderole** on each bottle as required.
- [ ] **Export**: EMCS for duty-suspended EU movement; per-market import/label rules for US (TTB
      COLA), UK, etc.

## 3. Trademark & IP  *(do this before printing bottles)*

- [ ] Trademark search + filing — **EUIPO** (EU-wide) and Croatia (**DZIV**).
- [ ] File in priority export markets (USPTO/TTB for US, UKIPO for UK).
- [ ] Register **domain(s)** and **social handles** for the chosen name.
- [ ] Keep "šljivovica/plum brandy" as a *descriptor*, brand name as the distinctive mark.

## 4. Labeling (legally mandatory content)

EU alcohol labels generally require:
- [ ] Product name / category (e.g. "Šljivovica — plum brandy")
- [ ] **ABV** (% vol) and **net volume** (e.g. 700 ml)
- [ ] Producer/bottler name & address
- [ ] **Lot/batch number**
- [ ] Allergen / energy info per current EU rules (check 2026 requirements)
- [ ] Excise stamp placement
- [ ] Responsible-drinking / pregnancy pictogram where required by market
> The provided SVG labels are *design* templates — drop the mandatory text into the footer zone
> I left for it before printing.

## 5. Packaging & suppliers

- [ ] **Bottle** — pick the silhouette per concept (tall slim for Korana; heavy heritage glass for
      Most 1792; matte-black for Vučja Suza). Source from an EU glass supplier (e.g. in HR/SI/IT).
- [ ] **Closure** — natural cork + capsule, or wax-dipped (Most 1792).
- [ ] **Label printing** — premium stock, foil/letterpress; get a print-shop dieline and match the SVG.
- [ ] **Secondary packaging** — gift tube/box for reserve SKUs.
- [ ] Get a **dieline** from your printer and adapt the SVG to it.

## 6. Pricing (premium positioning)

| Tier | Example | Target shelf price |
|---|---|---|
| Core white | Korana Bijela | €30–40 |
| Oak-aged | Korana Zlatna / Most Klasik | €45–65 |
| Reserve / numbered | Rezerva / Solera / Mjesec | €80–150+ |

Price *up*. Cheap rakija is everywhere; you are selling estate, place, and scarcity.

## 8. Route to market

- [ ] **Cellar-door / estate sales** + Plitvice tourist traffic (huge advantage — millions of
      visitors pass nearby).
- [ ] Croatian fine-spirits retailers & restaurants.
- [ ] EU export via a distributor; US via an importer (you cannot self-import to the US).
- [ ] Spirits competitions (medals = instant credibility for a new brand).
- [ ] Story-led PR: "single-estate brandy from the source of the Korana" is a press-ready angle.

## 9. First 90 days (suggested order)

1. Pick the concept + finalize name.
2. Trademark search → file. Grab domains/handles.
3. Confirm contract-distiller + this season's plum plan.
4. Company + excise registration with accountant.
5. Finalize label (mandatory text in), order glass + print samples.
6. Build the one-page site (already drafted in `site/`).
7. Bottle a small first batch; enter one competition; start cellar-door + tourist sales.
