## The shared truth (your "terroir story")

Every premium spirit sells a *place*. Yours is unusually good:

- **The village** — Ličko Petrovo Selo, founded **1792**, in Lika, Croatia's high, cold,
  thinly-peopled interior. Harsh winters, clean karst water, short fierce summers that
  concentrate sugar in the fruit.
- **The water** — the **Korana river** is born here, out of the Plitvice Lakes. The orchard
  drinks the same water that feeds a UNESCO World Heritage landscape.
- **The bridge** — the old stone crossing over the river is the village's landmark and your
  natural logo motif.
- **The land** — your mother is a citizen; the orchard is *family land*, not bought stock.
  This is the single most valuable phrase in premium spirits: **single-estate, family-owned.**
- **The craft** — Lika/Slavonian tradition: ripe plums fermented 3–4 weeks, copper-kettle
  distilled, the best run double-distilled into *prepečenica* and rested in Slavonian oak
  until it turns gold.

Lead with all of this. Cheap rakija is "strong." Premium rakija is "from *here*, by *us*, slowly."

# CONCEPT 1 — **KORANA**
### Modern minimalist luxury · "Born of the source"

**Positioning.** The clean, contemporary, design-forward bottle. Think Scandinavian-spirits
restraint applied to a Balkan tradition. Sells in design hotels, natural-wine bars, and to a
younger international buyer who'd never call it "moonshine."

**Name.** *Korana* — the river born at your doorstep. Short, soft, pronounceable in any language,
ownable as a trademark in spirits.

**Back story (label/web copy):**
> Where the Plitvice lakes spill over their last stone lip, a river is born. It is called the
> Korana. Our plums grow on its first bank, in Ličko Petrovo Selo, drinking the same water that
> carves the famous falls below. We pick by hand, ferment slow, and distill in copper. Nothing
> added, nothing hurried. **Korana — from the source.**

**Voice.** Spare. Confident. Lowercase headlines. Lots of white space. Never shouty.

**Palette.** Glacier white · deep Korana teal (`#0E5C63`) · cold silver foil. Optional single
turquoise dot (the river's source).

**Label concept (`labels/01-korana.svg`).** Tall, narrow, almost no border. A single fine line
that traces the river's bend. Brand name in a light modern serif, small. ABV and "single-estate
šljivovica" in tiny tracking-spaced caps at the foot.

**Range / SKUs.**
- *Korana Bijela* — unaged white plum, crystal clear, 42%
- *Korana Zlatna* — oak-rested gold, 43%
- *Korana Rezerva* — long-aged, numbered bottles, 45%

# CONCEPT 2 — **MOST 1792**
### Heritage & heraldry · "Cross the old bridge"

**Positioning.** The classic, ceremonial, gift-and-collector bottle. Engraved-feel, founding
year front and center, wax seal, heavy glass. Sells in duty-free, fine-spirits retail, and as
a prestige gift. This is the one that looks like it's been made for 200 years.

**Name.** *Most* means "bridge" in Croatian. **MOST 1792** ties the village landmark to the
founding year — instantly legitimate, hard to fake, easy to defend as a mark.

**Back story:**
> In 1792 the first families crossed the Korana and settled the far bank. They built a stone
> bridge and planted plums. Two centuries later the bridge still stands and the orchard still
> bears. Each autumn we cross it with empty baskets and return with the fruit that becomes this
> brandy. **MOST 1792 — every bottle crosses the same bridge.**

**Voice.** Formal, warm, a little ceremonial. Croatian and English side by side. Uses "Est. 1792."

**Palette.** Aged ivory · oxblood/burgundy (`#5A1A1A`) · antique gold foil · black. A real or
faux **wax seal** in burgundy.

**Label concept (`labels/02-most-1792.svg`).** Engraved line-art of the stone bridge arch over
the river. Ornate border frame, "ESTABLISHED 1792" banner, blackletter-tinged serif wordmark,
a crest where the bridge keystone sits. Neck label with wax-seal motif.

**Range / SKUs.**
- *Most 1792 Klasik* — flagship oak-aged šljivovica, 42%
- *Most 1792 Prepečenica* — double-distilled, 52%, for the connoisseur
- *Most 1792 Solera* — barrel-blended reserve, gift tube

# CONCEPT 3 — **VUČJA SUZA**  *(Wolf's Tear)*
### Wild, romantic, dark prestige · "Lika is wolf country"

**Positioning.** The storytelling bottle with edge and soul — the one bartenders fall in love
with and write about. Lika is genuinely famous for wolves and bears; the name is evocative,
unforgettable, and unmistakably *of this place*. Sells on narrative and personality.

**Name.** *Vučja Suza* — "Wolf's Tear." A drop of something wild and pure. (English fallback /
co-brand: **WOLF'S TEAR**.) Mythic, memorable, deeply ownable.

**Back story:**
> They say the highlands of Lika belong to the wolf. In the cold months you hear them across the
> Korana. Our plums ripen under the same hard sky — short summers, black winters, water clean
> enough to drink from the stone. The fruit fights to be sweet. What we distill from it is wild
> at heart and clear as a tear. **Vučja Suza — the wild drop of Lika.**

**Voice.** Lyrical, cinematic, a touch mythic. Short sentences that land like folklore.

**Palette.** Charcoal/near-black · moonlight silver · frost blue (`#9BB7C4`) · a single blood-plum
violet (`#5B2A4A`). Matte black bottle, silver line-art.

**Label concept (`labels/03-vucja-suza.svg`).** A wolf's head rendered in a single continuous
silver line that doubles as a river / a falling tear. Minimal type, lots of black. Feels like a
tattoo and a luxury fragrance at once.

**Range / SKUs.**
- *Vučja Suza* — flagship clear plum, 44%
- *Vučja Suza Crna* ("Black") — oak-aged, charcoal-filtered, 46%
- *Vučja Suza Mjesec* ("Moon") — limited winter-distilled run, numbered

## My recommendation

- **Going for prestige + broad export appeal →** start with **KORANA**. Cleanest to trademark,
  easiest on foreign tongues, and the design scales from a €35 bottle to a €120 reserve.
- **Going for gift / collector / duty-free →** **MOST 1792**. The founding year does heavy lifting
  and the format justifies a high price.
- **Going for a cult, story-led brand bartenders champion →** **VUČJA SUZA**. Highest ceiling on
  *love*, slightly higher risk on mass-market recognition.

You can also run **KORANA as the house brand** and use **MOST 1792 / VUČJA SUZA as special editions**
under it. That's a very common premium-spirits structure and lets you test all three.

## Naming & legal notes (read before you print anything)

- "Šljivovica" / "Slivovitz" is a **protected geographical-style term**; you can use it descriptively
  ("plum brandy / šljivovica") but your *brand name* must be the distinctive part. All three above are.
- Check trademark availability in: **EUIPO** (EU mark), Croatia (DZIV), and any export market (e.g.
  US TTB/USPTO, UK). Do this *before* spending on bottles and foil.
- Secure domains + social handles for your chosen name early (see launch checklist).
